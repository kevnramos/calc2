Separation of Concerns

Basics

Separation of concerns is a design idea used in computer science to divide a computer program into seperate components. Each part focuses on a different issue.


Perfect Example

The perfect example for separation of concerns is a number of functions in a class.

These separate functions break down a program into simpler chunks, which lowers the chance for something to go wrong, and makes it easier to fix and spot bugs.
